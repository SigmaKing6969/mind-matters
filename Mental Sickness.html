Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear, worry, or nervousness. Common types include Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder, and Social Anxiety Disorder.

Mood Disorders

Mood disorders primarily affect a personâ€™s emotional state. Examples include Major Depressive Disorder, Bipolar Disorder, and Cyclothymic Disorder.

Personality Disorders

Personality disorders involve enduring patterns of behavior, cognition, and inner experience. Examples include Borderline Personality Disorder, Antisocial Personality Disorder, and Narcissistic Personality Disorder.

Psychotic Disorders

Psychotic disorders involve distorted thinking and awareness. The most common example is Schizophrenia, which can include hallucinations and delusions.

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are serious conditions related to persistent eating behaviors. Examples include Anorexia Nervosa, Bulimia Nervosa, and Binge-Eating Disorder.

Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders

These disorders include Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) and related conditions like Body Dysmorphic Disorder and Hoarding Disorder.

Trauma and Stressor-Related Disorders

These disorders are related to experiencing or witnessing trauma. Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a common example.
